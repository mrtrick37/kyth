# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

KythOS is a community-facing project, even when much of the work starts from
one person's daily-driver needs. We pledge to make participation a respectful,
welcoming experience for contributors, testers, users, and maintainers.

## Expected Behavior

- Be kind, patient, and specific.
- Assume good faith while still naming risks clearly.
- Respect different experience levels, hardware setups, and use cases.
- Keep bug reports and reviews focused on the work.
- Avoid sharing private information, credentials, logs with secrets, or personal
  data that is not necessary to the issue.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, threats, insults, or sustained disruption.
- Sexualized language or imagery.
- Publishing another person's private information without explicit permission.
- Bad-faith security testing against systems you do not own or control.
- Dismissing reasonable safety, accessibility, privacy, or security concerns.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers may remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, issues, pull
requests, and other contributions that do not align with this Code of Conduct.
Maintainers may temporarily or permanently limit participation for behavior that
they determine is inappropriate, threatening, or harmful.

To report a conduct concern, contact the project maintainer through GitHub. For
security vulnerabilities, use the private reporting process in `SECURITY.md`
instead of a public issue.
